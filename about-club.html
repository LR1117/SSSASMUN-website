Our Story (The Honest Version)

We didn't start out revolutionary. We just liked arguing about geopolitics.

What We Actually Do

We simulate the UN. Your job: represent a country, debate complex international issues, and try to persuade other delegates that your position isn't completely ridiculous. Spoiler: Sometimes it is, and that's the fun part.

The Real Talk

We're not about empty corporate mission statements. We're about building students who understand the world, can articulate their thoughts under pressure, and somehow make teenage diplomacy look interesting to their parents.

How We Got Here

First Conference

The Club Experience

Thursdays at 3:30 PM

We meet. We learn. We argue about whether Estonia's foreign policy makes sense (it does, FYI).

Conference Season

Spring and fall, we pack our bags and go compete. You'll make friends, win trophies, and probably get emotionally invested in a fake country's politics.

Late Night Study Sessions

Position papers, research, strategy. We've had some of our best ideas at 11 PM on a Wednesday.

Leadership Roles

Start as a delegate. Become an officer. Train new members. It's a pipeline, and it's weirdly satisfying.

Why People Stick Around

1.

The People — You'll meet the weird, brilliant kids from 10 different schools who also think geopolitics is interesting.

2.

The Growth — By year two, you'll be unrecognizable. Speaking skills? Confidence? Understanding of world affairs? All dramatically improved.

3.

The Wins — Whether it's your first award or your tenth, it hits differently when you know you earned it.

4.

The Inside Jokes — You'll have 50+ people who get the weirdest references from last year's conferences.
