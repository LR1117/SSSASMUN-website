About Our Model UN Club

Building tomorrow's diplomats and global leaders

🎯

Our Mission

To foster understanding of international affairs, develop critical thinking skills, and provide students with a platform to engage in meaningful debate on global issues through simulation of United Nations proceedings.

✨

Our Vision

To cultivate a diverse community of informed, articulate, and passionate students who are prepared to tackle global challenges and become leaders in their communities and the world.

📚

Our Values

Respect, Diplomacy, Collaboration, Integrity, and Excellence. We believe in civil discourse, diverse perspectives, and the power of education to change the world.

Club History

Our Journey and Growth

2020 - Club Founded

Our Model UN club was established with a small group of passionate students interested in international relations and debate. We began with regular meetings and practice simulations.

2021 - First Conference

We attended our first regional Model UN conference, where our delegates competed against schools from across the state. This experience energized our club and inspired growth.

2022 - Expansion

Our membership grew significantly as we held training workshops and recruited new members. We attended multiple conferences and won several awards for outstanding delegations.

2023 - Recognition

Our club was recognized by the school as one of the top student organizations. We expanded our curriculum to include specialized committees and advanced training programs.

2024 - Present

Today, we continue to grow and develop with members competing at state and national conferences. We're committed to fostering excellence in diplomacy and global awareness.

What We Do

Club Activities and Opportunities

01

Weekly Meetings

Join us every week for discussions, debates, and training sessions where we prepare for conferences and discuss current international events.

02

Conference Participation

We attend regional, state, and national Model UN conferences where our delegates represent assigned countries in simulated UN committees.

03

Research & Preparation

Members conduct in-depth research on assigned countries and topics, developing position papers and strategic diplomatic approaches.

04

Leadership Development

Through various roles including delegate, chair, and committee coordinator, members develop leadership and organizational skills.

05

Community Outreach

We host workshops and demos for younger students to introduce them to Model UN and inspire the next generation of diplomats.

06

Social Events

We build camaraderie through team-building activities, watch parties, and celebration events after successful conferences.

Club Structure

Leadership and Organization

President

Leads the club, organizes meetings, and represents the club in school activities.

Vice President

Assists the president and oversees committee assignments and member development.

Secretary

Maintains records, handles communications, and coordinates scheduling.

Treasurer

Manages club finances, conference fees, and fundraising activities.

Conference Director

Plans and coordinates conference attendance and delegate assignments.

Training Director

Develops curriculum and leads workshops for member skill development.

Committees We Participate In

Different Types of UN Simulations

General Assembly

The largest UN body where all member states participate. Debates cover a wide range of global issues including development, security, and humanitarian concerns.

Security Council

Focused on international peace and security. A smaller, more strategic committee where permanent members have significant influence.

Economic & Social Council (ECOSOC)

Addresses economic, social, cultural, and humanitarian issues. Focuses on sustainable development and human rights.

Specialized Committees

Committees addressing specific issues like the Environment, Science & Technology, Human Rights, and the Rights of the Child.
