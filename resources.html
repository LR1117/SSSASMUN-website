Research Like a Pro

You need to know your country better than the country knows itself. Not really, but close.

Primary Sources

  • UN official documents and resolutions
  • Country foreign policy statements
  • Congressional records and voting history
  • International news archives

Secondary Sources

  • Council on Foreign Relations analysis
  • Academic journals and think tanks
  • BBC, AP, Reuters news
  • Diplomatic briefings

Tools That Help

  • Google Scholar for academic papers
  • Worldometer for statistics
  • UN website for official docs
  • Wikipedia for quick overviews (seriously)

Advice from People Who've Been There

On Getting Started

"Your first conference will be overwhelming. That's normal. Go to every session, take notes, and don't worry about being perfect. Nobody expects you to be brilliant on day one. By day three, though, you should have your stuff together."

— A third-year delegate

On Choosing Countries

"Pick countries you genuinely want to learn about. Yes, representing the US or UK is easier, but representing Mongolia or Costa Rica? That's where the fun is. You'll learn more, and people will actually remember you."

— Conference Director

On Dealing with Competition

"You're competing against schools with dedicated Model UN classes and coaches. Don't panic. We win through preparation, strategy, and honestly just caring more. You can't fake caring, and it shows."

— Award winner

On The Late Night Study Sessions

"Some of our best ideas came at 11 PM the night before a conference. Exhausted brains are creative brains. Also, bring snacks. Lots of snacks."

— Someone who's been there

Different Flavors of Model UN

General Assembly

The big one. Everyone's involved. Chaotic. Unpredictable. Honestly the best.

Security Council

Smaller group, more strategy, permanent members get power. Think chess with countries.

Crisis Committees

Everything goes wrong in real time. You have to improvise. Stressful but unforgettable.

Specialized Committees

Human rights, environment, trade. More focused, less chaos, still engaging.

Questions People Actually Ask

Do I have to memorize everything about my country?

No. But you should know the major points cold. There's a difference between knowing and memorizing. Know your stuff without sounding like a robot.

What if I completely bomb my first speech?

Welcome to 99% of delegates' first experiences. Everyone bombs. You learn. You get better. By conference three, you'll be solid.

Is being good at debate required?

Not at all. We have members who've never debated. They learned Model UN from scratch. Some are now our best delegates.

How much does this actually cost?

Membership is free. Conferences are $50-150 depending on where. Sometimes we fundraise to help members go.

Can I join in the middle of the year?

Absolutely. We get new members in January all the time. You'll be ready for spring conference.

What if I'm terrified of public speaking?

You'll be less terrified after one conference. By year two, you might actually enjoy it. That's not guaranteed, but it's likely.
