Research Guide

Primary Research Sources

  • UN Official Website: www.un.org - Official resolutions, data, and publications
  • CIA World Factbook: Detailed information about countries and regions
  • Council on Foreign Relations: Analysis of international issues
  • United Nations Documents: Access to all official UN proceedings

Research Strategy

  1. Understand your committee's agenda topics
  2. Research your assigned country's position on each topic
  3. Find current events and historical context
  4. Identify potential alliances with other countries
  5. Develop your country's speaking points and solutions

Key Documents to Prepare

  • Position Paper (required for most conferences)
  • Country Profile and Foreign Policy Overview
  • Voting Record on Similar Issues
  • Speaking Notes and Key Arguments

Writing Guide

Position Paper Structure

  1. Header: Country name, committee, topic
  2. Introduction: Hook and overview of position
  3. Background: Historical context and current situation
  4. Country Position: Detailed stance on the issue
  5. Solutions: Proposed actions and policies
  6. Conclusion: Summary and call to action

Writing Tips

  • Use professional and diplomatic language
  • Support arguments with credible sources and statistics
  • Maintain your country's perspective throughout
  • Use active voice and clear, concise sentences
  • Proofread multiple times for grammar and spelling
  • Keep paragraphs focused on single ideas

Learn the proper formatting for UN resolutions including preamble clauses, operative clauses, and sponsor/co-sponsor requirements.

Debate & Speaking Guide

Speaking Effectively in Committee

  • Maintain eye contact with the chair and other delegates
  • Speak clearly and at a moderate pace
  • Use hand gestures to emphasize key points
  • Begin with formal address: "The delegation of [Country]..."
  • Reference other delegates' points to engage in debate
  • Stay calm and diplomatic, even in disagreement

Debate Strategies

  • Build Consensus: Form alliances with like-minded nations
  • Counter Arguments: Anticipate opposing views and prepare rebuttals
  • Use Evidence: Back up claims with facts and statistics
  • Listen Actively: Pay attention to other delegates' positions
  • Stay in Character: Always speak from your country's perspective
  • Be Flexible: Adjust strategy based on committee dynamics

Common Speaking Phrases

  • "We respectfully disagree..."
  • "In light of this evidence..."
  • "The delegation wishes to yield the floor to..."
  • "We propose an alternative approach..."
  • "Building on the previous speaker's point..."

Conference Strategy

Pre-Conference Preparation

  • Review all committee topics and background guides
  • Complete thorough country research
  • Write position papers 2+ weeks before conference
  • Practice speeches and debate responses
  • Connect with other delegates from your school
  • Pack all necessary documents and materials

During Conference

  • Attend all sessions and committee meetings
  • Take detailed notes on other countries' positions
  • Network with delegates from other schools
  • Negotiate and form strategic alliances
  • Be ready to modify positions based on new information
  • Participate actively in resolution writing

Winning Awards

  • Best Delegate: Active participation, strong research, persuasive speaking
  • Outstanding Delegation: Teamwork, cooperation, overall performance
  • Best Position Paper: Depth of research, clear writing, original ideas
  • Honorable Mention: Consistent quality throughout conference

Frequently Asked Questions

Answers to common Model UN questions

Do I need prior debate experience?

No! Model UN welcomes members of all experience levels. We provide training and support for beginners.

How much does it cost to join?

Club membership is free. Conference fees ($50-150) are optional and cover transportation and registration.

How much time does Model UN take?

Weekly meetings are 1-2 hours. Conference preparation varies, but expect 2-3 hours weekly before conferences.

What if I've never done public speaking?

Model UN is a great way to build confidence! We teach speaking skills through training and practice sessions.

Can I join mid-year?

Absolutely! We welcome new members at any time during the school year.

How are delegates assigned to countries?

Country assignments are made by our Conference Director based on member preferences and availability.
